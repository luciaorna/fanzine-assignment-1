FANZINE "PUNK PLANET"

Introduction to the fanzine

Founded in 1994 by Dan Sinker, Punk Planet was a Chicago-based zine that redefined the boundaries of the underground. While many of its contemporaries focused strictly on the sonic aspects of the scene, this publication treated punk as a comprehensive DIY philosophy. It prioritized progressive politics, visual arts, and social activism, offering a sophisticated platform for voices often sidelined by the mainstream, including the Riot Grrrl movement and the LGBTQ+ community.

The zine was celebrated for its clean, professional aesthetic which contrasted with the traditional "cut-and-paste" look of early punk media, proving that independent journalism could be both gritty and polished. Throughout its 80-issue run, it served as a vital cultural compass until its closure in 2007. Today, it remains a landmark of independent publishing, remembered for its commitment to the idea that being "punk" meant engaging critically with the world rather than just wearing the right leather jacket.
